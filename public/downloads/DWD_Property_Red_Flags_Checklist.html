Access & Location Issues
Property has no road access or requires crossing private land
Located in flood zone or high-risk area
Surrounded by industrial facilities or waste sites
In area with declining property values
No utilities available (water, sewer, electric)
Why this matters:
Properties without proper access or in undesirable locations are difficult to sell and may require expensive improvements to make them marketable.
Environmental Hazards
Property listed on environmental databases (EPA, state records)
Evidence of underground storage tanks
Asbestos or lead-based paint in older structures
Mold or water damage visible
Nearby contamination sources (gas stations, dry cleaners)
Why this matters:
Environmental issues can cost tens of thousands to remediate and may make the property unsellable until cleaned up.

Zoning & Legal Restrictions
Zoning doesn't allow intended use (residential vs commercial)
Property subject to HOA restrictions or fees
Easements that limit development or access
Historic preservation restrictions
Covenants that run with the land
Why this matters:
Legal restrictions can prevent you from using the property as intended or require expensive compliance measures.
Structural & Condition Issues
Foundation cracks or settling visible
Roof damage or missing shingles
Electrical or plumbing systems outdated
HVAC system missing or non-functional
Windows and doors need replacement
Why this matters:
Major structural or system repairs can easily exceed the property's value, especially in lower-priced tax sale properties.
Financial Red Flags
Property taxes exceed 2% of assessed value annually
Outstanding liens or judgments against property
HOA fees over $200/month
Special assessments pending
Insurance costs unusually high
Why this matters:
High ongoing costs can make a property unprofitable even if acquired cheaply at auction.
