One Week Before Auction
Confirm auction date, time, and location
Complete final property research and due diligence
Calculate and finalize maximum bid amounts
Review auction rules and registration requirements
Arrange for required deposit or earnest money
Verify acceptable payment methods
Day Before Auction
Drive by property one final time
Prepare cashier's check or verify bank transfer capability
Organize all documents: ID, proof of funds, registration
Print property research notes and bid sheets
Get good night's sleep
Pro Tip:
Arrive at least 30 minutes early to register, observe the process, and identify competing bidders.
Auction Day Morning
Dress professionally and bring comfortable shoes
Bring required documents in organized folder
Arrive 45-60 minutes early
Complete registration process
Get bidder number or paddle
What to Bring
Government-issued photo ID
Cashier's check or proof of funds
Property research notes and maximum bid sheet
Calculator
Pen and notepad
Copy of auction rules
Attorney's contact information (if needed)
During the Auction
Listen carefully to auctioneer's announcements
Stay calm and disciplined with your maximum bid
Bid confidently and clearly
Never exceed your predetermined maximum
Take notes on winning bids for future reference
Critical Rule:
NEVER exceed your maximum bid, no matter how excited you get. Emotion is the enemy of profit.
If You Win
Complete payment immediately per auction rules
Get receipt and all documentation
Confirm deed delivery timeline
Contact attorney to begin title work
Secure property and change locks if applicable
If You Don't Win
Note the winning bid amount for future reference
Stay for entire auction to observe and learn
Network with other investors
Research next auction opportunities
